03 Findings, ranked by what to fix first

criticalAI crawler access in robots.txtAgent access

robots.txt blocks: GPTBot, PerplexityBot. Those assistants cannot legally read this store — you are invisible in their shopping answers.

Fix: Remove the Disallow rules for AI crawler user-agents you want sales from.

highAvailability in schemaStructured data

No availability field. Agents deprioritize products they can't confirm are in stock.

Fix: Emit offers.availability (schema.org/InStock etc.) per variant.

highRatings exposed (aggregateRating)Structured data

Reviews are not exposed as aggregateRating schema. To an agent, this store has zero social proof — even if you have hundreds of reviews.

Fix: Have your reviews app emit aggregateRating/review JSON-LD on product pages.

mediumSubstantive product descriptionsCatalog clarity

50% of products have ≥40 words of real description (3/6). Agents answer questions (origin, fit, materials) straight from this text — thin copy means wrong or no answers.

Fix: Rewrite thin product descriptions; cover the questions a shopper would ask a clerk.

mediumReturn policy with explicit windowTrust signals

Return policy exists but no explicit day-count window found.

Fix: Publish /policies/refund-policy with a concrete window (e.g. “30 days”).

mediumSKU / GTIN identifiersStructured data

No SKU/GTIN in schema. Agents use identifiers to dedupe and compare across stores; without them you lose comparison placements.

Fix: Populate sku (and gtin where products have barcodes) in Product JSON-LD.

mediumProduct types categorizedCatalog clarity

50% of products carry a product_type. Category fields feed agents' filters (“show me pour-over gear under $40”).

Fix: Set product_type on every product; use consistent values.

mediumShipping policy with concrete timeframeTrust signals

Shipping policy exists but never states how many days delivery takes.

Fix: Publish /policies/shipping-policy with explicit delivery windows.

mediumllms.txt guidance fileAgent access

No llms.txt. Optional but cheap: a plain-text map of your store for language models.

Fix: Publish /llms.txt with your top collections, policies, and brand one-liner.

mediumBrand declaredStructured data

No brand in Product schema — hurts entity matching when a shopper asks for you by name.

Fix: Add brand to Product JSON-LD.

mediumOrganization schema on homepageStructured data

No Organization schema on the homepage — weakens brand entity recognition.

Fix: Add Organization JSON-LD (name, url, logo, sameAs socials).

mediumCanonical URLs on product pagesStructured data

No canonical link — variant/collection URL duplicates can split your product's identity across several URLs.

Fix: Emit rel=canonical on product pages.

mediumHomepage meta descriptionStructured data

“Welcome to our online store!”

Fix: Write a 50–160 character description that says what you sell and for whom.

mediumProducts tagged with attributesCatalog clarity

17% of products have tags (roast level, origin, size…). Tags become filterable attributes in agent queries.

Fix: Tag products with the attributes shoppers filter by.

mediumContact page reachableTrust signals

No /pages/contact. A reachable human is part of agents' merchant-quality scoring.

Fix: Publish a contact page with a real email or form.

Method. Agentrank reads only what an AI shopping agent can read: the public catalog feed (/products.json), robots.txt directives for seven AI crawlers, JSON-LD structured data on the homepage and product pages, variant and category metadata, and published shipping/return/contact policies. Every check maps to a step where an agent either ranks, describes, or completes a purchase from a store. No login, no private data — if Agentrank can't see it, neither can the agent.
